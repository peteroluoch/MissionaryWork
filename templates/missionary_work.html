RCCG Solution Centre, Nairobi

The RCCG Solution Centre in Nairobi was established by Pastor Prince as a hub for spiritual growth, community outreach, and social development. Located on Raila Odinga Way, opposite Armed Forces Memorial Hospital, the centre has become a beacon of hope for many in the community.

The centre offers:

  • Regular worship services and prayer meetings
  • Counseling and spiritual guidance
  • Community outreach programs
  • Youth development initiatives

Turkana County Initiatives

Pastor Prince has developed a special focus on Turkana County, one of the most marginalized regions in Kenya. His work in this area has been transformative, addressing both spiritual and physical needs of the community.

Key initiatives in Turkana include:

  • Mentoring program for local residents, providing guidance and support
  • Drilling of boreholes to provide clean water to communities
  • Church planting in remote areas to bring the gospel to unreached communities
  • Educational scholarships for children from disadvantaged backgrounds

South Sudan

Pastor Prince's recent mission to South Sudan in April 2025 was a tremendous success, with 1,138 souls receiving salvation during mission activities in Kapoeta. Despite challenging conditions, including heavy rain, the team persevered in preaching the gospel across several occasions.

Key Achievements:

  • 1,138 souls saved during the April 2025 mission in Kapoeta
  • Church building construction in Nanimtoit, Kapoeta North
  • Gospel outreach to multiple communities despite heavy rainfall
  • Provision of essential resources, clean water, and medical assistance

Gospel Outreach in Kapoeta

The mission team conducted multiple gospel outreach events across Kapoeta, reaching thousands with the message of Christ. Despite heavy rain on several occasions, the preaching continued, and many souls were saved.

Outreach activities included:

  • Open-air crusades in multiple locations
  • Door-to-door evangelism
  • Youth and children's programs
  • Training for local church leaders

Church Building Project

One of the key objectives of the mission was to start church building construction in Nanimtoit, Kapoeta North. Although the team faced some challenges, including river conditions, they made significant progress in establishing a foundation for the local church.

The church building project includes:

  • Site selection and preparation
  • Community involvement in construction
  • Training local leaders for church management
  • Provision of building materials and resources

Mission Diary Highlights

Day 1: Arrival

Our team arrived in Kapoeta and was warmly welcomed by local leaders. We spent the day setting up our base and preparing for the mission activities ahead.

Day 2: First Outreach

We conducted our first gospel outreach event in the central market area of Kapoeta. Despite the challenges, many people gathered to hear the message, and 87 souls gave their lives to Christ.

Day 3: Church Site

We met with local leaders to select a site for the church building in Nanimtoit. After prayer and consultation, a suitable location was identified, and initial preparations began.

Day 4: Nanimtoit

We took the message of Christ to the people of Nanimtoit, Kapoeta North. Despite heavy rain, the gospel was preached, and many souls were saved.

Rwanda

Pastor Prince has led missions to Rwanda to minister to widowed survivors of the genocide, pray with them, and offer assistance. His work in Rwanda focuses on healing, reconciliation, and community support in a country that has experienced significant trauma.

Key Achievements:

  • Ministry to widowed survivors of the genocide, providing spiritual and emotional support
  • Establishment of prayer and support groups in multiple communities
  • Provision of material assistance, including food, clothing, and shelter to those in need
  • Gospel outreach and church planting in areas with limited access to spiritual resources

Healing and Reconciliation Ministry

Pastor Prince's work in Rwanda has been particularly focused on bringing healing and reconciliation to communities affected by the 1994 genocide. Through prayer, counseling, and community support, he has helped many find peace and forgiveness.

The healing ministry includes:

  • Regular prayer meetings for widows and survivors
  • Trauma counseling and emotional support
  • Reconciliation workshops bringing together different community groups
  • Bible studies focused on forgiveness and healing

Community Development

Beyond spiritual support, Pastor Prince has been involved in practical community development initiatives in Rwanda, helping survivors rebuild their lives and communities.

Community development projects include:

  • Vocational training for widows to help them become self-sufficient
  • Microfinance initiatives to support small businesses
  • Educational support for orphans and vulnerable children
  • Housing assistance for those who lost their homes
